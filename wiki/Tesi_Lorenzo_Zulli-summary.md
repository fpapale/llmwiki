**Summary of the Thesis – “Utilizzo di sistemi di AI Generativa per il design di un prodotto innovativo”**

| Section | Key Points |
|---|---|
| **Introduction** | • AI generativa can create new content (text, images, audio, video, 3D models) from large datasets.<br>• The thesis aims to design an innovative product by chaining multiple generative‑AI services. |
| **Chapter 1 – IA Generativa** | • Definitions, history (from 1950s to modern GANs, Transformers, LLMs).<br>• Discusses laws, ethical risks (bias, hallucinations, copyright).<br>• Explains core techniques: discriminative vs. generative models, GPT/Decoder‑Only, Encoder‑Only, Seq2Seq, MoE, MoME, quantization, distillation. |
| **Chapter 2 – Taxonomy of Generative AI** | • Categorises services by output type: <br> - **Text** (BLOOM, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, LLaMA, etc.)<br> - **Code** (GitHub Copilot, Codex, CodeT5, etc.)<br> - **Images** (DALL·E, Stable Diffusion, Midjourney, Flux, Imagen, …)<br> - **Audio/Music** (EnCodec, VALL‑E, Whisper, etc.)<br> - **Video** (KLING, Sora, Make‑A‑Video, MoCoGAN, …)<br> - **3D Models** (DreamFusion, GET3D, Kaedim, Rodin Hyper3D, …)<br> - **Synthetic Data** (Microsoft Fabric, Mostly AI, Synthea, …) |
| **Chapter 3 – Idea Generation with Google Gemini** | • Five conversation scenarios (generic, random, human‑guided, design‑thinking).<br>• Applied **Design Thinking** & **Double‑Diamond** phases (Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype).<br>• Produced personas, problem statements, “How‑might‑we” questions, and a list of product requirements (audio quality, robustness, modularity, safety, UI, etc.). |
| **Chapter 4 – Visualisation with DALL·E** | • Used the 10 Gemini‑derived prompts to generate 10 renders.<br>• Analysed DALL·E outputs: consistent style, occasional bias (e.g., default minute‑time values), occasional mismatches (missing features, colour errors). |
| **Chapter 5 – Video Mock‑ups with KLING** | • Created 5 × 360° video clips from the selected DALL·E render.<br>• Observed KLING’s behaviour (consistent rotation direction, occasional duplicated or missing buttons, background colour changes). |
| **Chapter 6 – 3‑D Modelling with Rodin Hyper3D** | • Produced 5 3‑D models from the same renders.<br>• Models were largely faithful; minor artefacts (mis‑placed tags, “game‑ready” label). |
| **Conclusions** | • Demonstrated a full **generative‑AI‑driven product design pipeline** (idea → text → image → video → 3‑D).<br>• Highlighted recurring **biases & hallucinations** across all services.<br>• Suggested future work: physical prototyping, refinement of 3‑D models, deeper bias mitigation, and broader ethical‑regulatory frameworks. |
| **Potential Extensions** | • 3‑D printing of refined models.<br>• Integration of electronics for a functional prototype.<br>• Systematic evaluation of AI‑generated artefacts against user‑centered design metrics.<br>• Development of standards for responsible generative‑AI use in product design. |

*Overall, the thesis showcases how multiple generative AI tools can be orchestrated to conceive, visualise, and prototype an innovative MP3‑player‑like device, while critically analysing the limitations and ethical considerations of each AI service.*
